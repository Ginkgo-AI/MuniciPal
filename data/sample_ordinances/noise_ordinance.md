# Municipal Noise Ordinance

## Section 1: Quiet Hours

Quiet hours are in effect from 10:00 PM to 7:00 AM on weekdays (Monday through Friday) and from 10:00 PM to 9:00 AM on weekends and federal holidays. During quiet hours, noise levels shall not exceed 55 decibels (dBA) at the property line of any residential zone.

During non-quiet hours, noise levels shall not exceed 70 decibels (dBA) at the property line of any residential zone and 80 decibels (dBA) in commercial or industrial zones.

All noise measurements shall be taken using a sound level meter meeting ANSI Type II standards at a height of four feet above ground level at the nearest property boundary.

## Section 2: Exemptions

The following activities are exempt from the noise limits established in Section 1:

1. Emergency vehicles and equipment operated by police, fire, or emergency medical services in the course of official duties.
2. Construction activity performed between 7:00 AM and 6:00 PM on weekdays, provided a valid construction permit has been issued by the Building Department.
3. Municipal operations including but not limited to street sweeping, snow removal, trash collection, and utility maintenance.
4. Parades, festivals, and public gatherings that have obtained a Special Event Permit from the City Clerk's office at least 30 days prior to the event.
5. Agricultural activities on parcels zoned for agricultural use within established operating hours.
6. Lawn maintenance equipment operated between 8:00 AM and 8:00 PM.

Requests for temporary noise variances for activities not listed above may be submitted to the Zoning Board of Appeals. Variance applications must be filed at least 14 business days before the planned activity and require a $75 filing fee.

## Section 3: Violations and Penalties

Any person found in violation of this ordinance shall be subject to the following penalties:

- First offense: Written warning with a compliance deadline of 24 hours.
- Second offense within 12 months: Fine of $150.
- Third offense within 12 months: Fine of $350.
- Fourth and subsequent offenses within 12 months: Fine of $500 and referral to Municipal Court.

Noise complaints may be filed with the Code Enforcement Office by calling 555-0100 or submitting a complaint online at the municipal website. The Code Enforcement Officer shall investigate complaints within 48 hours during business days.

Repeated violations (four or more within a 12-month period) may result in additional enforcement action including injunctive relief sought through Municipal Court.

This ordinance was adopted by the City Council on March 15, 2023, and shall take effect 30 days after publication.
